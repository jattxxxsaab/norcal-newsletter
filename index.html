📰 Top Stories

What You Need to Know

Interest Rates

Mortgage Rates Hit 3-Year Low — 6.00% on the 30-Year Fixed

Freddie Mac's March 5 survey shows the 30-year fixed averaging 6.00%, the lowest since early 2023. Bankrate reports 6.15%, Forbes 6.25% — but the trend is clear: rates are finally dropping. The Fed's stance on potential cuts later in 2026 is giving the bond market room to breathe.

For a $500K loan, this means roughly $200/month in savings compared to last year's 6.7% rates. That's $2,400/year back in buyers' pockets — enough to move the needle on affordability in the Central Valley.

🎯 Takeaway: This is the rate environment buyers have been waiting for. If you've had clients on the fence, now is the time to re-engage. Sub-6% may still be months away, but 6% is a real inflection point for buyer psychology.
Market Data

Bay Area Hits Record Median Price — But the Story Is Uneven

The Bay Area recorded its highest-ever median home price in February 2026 at $1.265M across Alameda, Contra Costa, San Francisco, and San Mateo counties. SF itself is up 9% at the start of the year with demand outstripping supply.

But San Jose tells a different story: median prices are down 10.3% YoY to $1.3M. New listings in the Bay Area hit their highest level in four years, only 12% below the all-time record. The market is splitting — well-priced single-family homes are still getting multiple offers, while overpriced listings and condos are sitting.

🎯 Takeaway: "Bay Area" is no longer one market. SF and the Peninsula are running hot; South Bay is resetting. Investors and agents need to think hyper-local right now.
🚨 New This Year

AI Photos in Listings Must Now Be Disclosed (AB 723)

As of January 1, 2026, AB 723 requires real estate agents and brokers to clearly disclose when listing photos have been digitally altered or AI-generated. If images materially change a property's appearance — think virtual staging that removes cracks, adds landscaping, or alters room sizes — the listing must flag it and provide access to the original, unedited photo.

🎯 Takeaway: If you're using AI staging tools (and you should be — they're great), make sure your disclosure language is tight. Add a simple note like "Photos include virtual staging" and keep originals on file. Don't risk a complaint over a missing disclaimer.
Investment Spotlight

Sacramento Approaching Balanced Market — First Time Since 2019

Sacramento's inventory has climbed to 2.9 months' supply in Q1 2026, flirting with the 3-month threshold that defines a balanced market. A projected 10% jump in Q2 listings could push it over the line by spring. Median prices are down 3.1% YoY to $468K — the first meaningful correction in years.

For investors, this means better entry points and more room to negotiate. For agents, it means coaching sellers to price competitively from day one — overpriced listings in Sac are sitting 60+ days now.

🎯 Takeaway: Sacramento under $470K is looking increasingly attractive for buy-and-hold investors. With UC Davis expansion and state government jobs providing a stable demand floor, the correction creates opportunity.
🏙️ Central Valley

Mountain House Is Officially a City — What It Means for Real Estate

Mountain House officially incorporated as California's newest city following its March 2024 election, with cityhood taking effect in July 2025. The community is already seeing the impact: median list prices jumped to $1.05M in February 2026, up from $988K in 2025. Sales volume surged from 100 to 269 homes sold.

Cityhood means local control over zoning, development fees, and infrastructure — which could accelerate new construction phases. The flip side: potential new city taxes and fees as the government builds out.

🎯 Takeaway: Mountain House is transitioning from bedroom community to real city. Long-term this is bullish for property values — local governance means better services and planning. Watch for new development announcements.

💡 Agent Pro Tip of the Week

With AB 723 now in effect, build an AI disclosure workflow into your listing process today. Use virtual staging — it's one of the highest-ROI tools in your toolkit — but create a simple checklist: (1) take and archive original photos, (2) add "Virtually staged" watermark or caption to altered images, (3) include disclosure language in MLS remarks. The agents who get ahead of compliance avoid the headaches later. Bonus: transparent listings actually build more buyer trust.
