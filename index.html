What You Need to Know

🚨 Action Required

New FinCEN Rules for Real Estate — Now in Effect

As of March 1, 2026, FinCEN's Residential Real Estate Reporting Rules are live. If you hold property through an LLC, partnership, or trust and do an all-cash transfer to another entity, the closing agent must now file a report with the Treasury.

Key exemptions: transfers due to death, divorce, court orders, and individual-to-own-trust transfers (standard estate planning funding deeds) are exempt. But gifts to someone else's trust or entity-to-entity transfers may trigger reporting.

🎯 Takeaway: If you have investment LLCs, talk to your attorney before your next transfer. This affects flippers, investors, and anyone moving properties between entities.
Market Data

CA Housing Feb 2026: Inventory Rising, Demand Steady

C.A.R.'s February 2026 report shows 327 new listings per day statewide vs 198 closed sales — a growing inventory pipeline. Pending sales at 238/day suggest buyer demand remains healthy. 24.2% of REALTORS® reported closing a sale last week, up 0.4% from prior quarter.

However, only 12.4% of agents expect sales to increase next week, and just 5.9% expect prices to rise — both down from last quarter. Spring market may bring more balanced conditions.

🎯 Takeaway: We're entering a healthier market with more options for buyers. Sellers still have leverage in low-inventory areas like Tracy and San Jose, but the days of 15 offers are fading.
Interest Rates

Mortgage Rates Holding Steady Near 6.7%

The 30-year fixed rate is hovering around 6.71% this week, roughly flat from last month. The Fed has signaled potential rate cuts later in 2026, but inflation data remains mixed. Most economists expect rates to drift toward 6.0-6.5% by year-end if the economy cooperates.

🎯 Takeaway: Don't wait for 5% rates — they're not coming back anytime soon. Today's rates are the new normal. Buy now, refinance later.
Investment Spotlight

Central Valley: Still the Best Value Play in NorCal

With median prices in Modesto ($445K), Stockton ($475K), and Merced ($385K) — all with 5-7% annual appreciation — the Central Valley remains the strongest value play for investors in Northern California. Cap rates on multi-family properties are running 5.5-7%, well above Bay Area's 3-4%.

Commercial/industrial is hot too — truck yards, warehouses, and logistics properties are seeing increased demand as e-commerce and Central Valley growth drive distribution needs.

🎯 Takeaway: The Central Valley is where cash flow lives. Bay Area appreciation is slowing; CV appreciation is accelerating. Math favors the Valley right now.
Policy Watch

Sacramento's ADU Boom Continues

California's ADU-friendly laws continue to drive construction across NorCal. Sacramento County saw a 23% increase in ADU permits filed in Q4 2025 vs Q4 2024. With AB 1033 allowing ADUs to be sold separately as condos in participating cities, this is creating new investment opportunities for creative builders.

🎯 Takeaway: If you have a property with room for an ADU in the Central Valley, you're sitting on untapped equity. Build costs are $150-250K; value add is often $200-350K.
